## Davao City Overview

### Basic Facts (2017)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Region** | Davao Region (Region XI), Mindanao |
| **Population** | ~1.6 million (2015 census) |
| **Climate** | Tropical, outside typhoon belt |
| **Language** | Bisaya (Cebuano), Filipino, English |
| **Mayor** | Sara Duterte (daughter of President Rodrigo Duterte) |
| **Nickname** | "The Durian Capital of the Philippines" |

- **Safe reputation** — Strictest anti-smoking, drinking-in-public, and curfew laws in PH
- **Clean and orderly** — Compared to Manila, feels more disciplined
- **Growing economy** — IT/BPO sector expanding
- **Cultural pride** — Strong Mindanaoan identity

## Local Culture & Daily Life

### Language Notes
David and characters would realistically code-switch between:
- **Bisaya** — Casual, emotional moments
- **Filipino** — Formal, school settings
- **English** — Technical terms, academic contexts

**Common Bisaya Expressions:**
| Expression | Meaning | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "Unsa man?" | "What?" | Surprise, confusion |
| "Mao ba?" | "Really?" | Disbelief |
| "Lami!" | "Delicious!" | Complimenting food |
| "Ambot" | "I don't know" | Dismissive |
| "Sige" | "Okay/go ahead" | Agreement |
| "Ayaw na lang" | "Never mind" | Resignation |

### Food & Eating
- **Breakfast:** Sinangag (garlic rice), eggs, dried fish
- **Merienda (snack):** Pan de sal, puto, local fruits
- **Lunch/Dinner:** Rice always, ulam (viand), soup
- **Davao specialty:** Durian, pomelo, mangosteen
- **Affordable eateries:** Carinderias (small restaurants)

### Transportation
- **Jeepneys** — Main public transport, fixed routes, ~₱8-10 fare
- **Tricycles** — For short distances, barangay travel
- **Taxis** — Metered, safer than Manila's
- **School buses** — DCNHS may provide for SPA students

### Family & Home Life
**Typical Filipino family dynamics:**
- Multi-generational households common
- Respect for elders (using "po" and "opo")
- Mother often the household manager
- Lola (grandmother) has significant influence
- Tight-knit but unspoken emotional distances

## Sensory Details for Scene Writing

### Smells
- Durian from street vendors (pungent, divisive)
- Diesel from jeepneys
- Roasted corn (mais) from sidewalk stalls
- Sampaguita flowers
- Rain on hot pavement

### Sounds
- Jeepney horns and rumbling engines
- Roosters crowing (early morning)
- Karaoke from neighbor's house (evening)
- Church bells on Sundays
- Cicadas in summer heat

### Sights
- Green mountains visible from the city
- Colorful jeepneys painted with slogans
- Street vendors with umbrellas
- Students in white/colored uniforms
- Sari-sari stores on every corner

### Textures & Temperatures
- Humid air, sticky on skin
- Sudden afternoon rains (habagat season)
- Cool mornings (~24°C), hot afternoons (~32°C)
- Worn wooden pews in old churches
- Cold metal handles of practice room pianos

## Quick Reference: Scene Grounding

When writing a scene, include at least ONE:
1. **Local term** (Bisaya word or expression)
2. **Sensory detail** (smell, sound, temperature)
3. **Cultural marker** (food, transport, social custom)

This prevents the story from feeling generic and grounds it in Davao City, 2017.
