# Character Profiles: David Yoro — The Tenor's Second Chance

---

## Protagonist

### David Yoro

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Birthday** | March 19, 2000 |
| **Age (Regressed)** | 17 (Grade 11, 2017) |
| **Mental Age** | 37 |
| **Voice Type (Original)** | Baritone |
| **Voice Type (Now)** | Tenor (with training) |
| **Height** | 5'7" (170 cm) |
| **Build** | Lean, underdeveloped for opera |
| **Residence** | Mandug, San Jose Street (45 min commute) |
| **Vehicle** | Yamaha Sniper 135 (Underbone) |

#### Family Background
- **Mother:** Elena Yoro — Hardworking single mother, works as a government clerk. Quietly supportive but carries disappointment from David's original timeline failures.
- **Father:** Separated since David was young. Minimal contact.
- **Grandmother:** Lola Consuelo — The emotional anchor. Traditional Davaoeña who keeps the household together.
- **Grandfather (deceased):** Lolo Andres — Died 2009. Played banjo, 12-string guitar, and ukulele. David's earliest musical influence.

#### Technical Profile

| Strength | Weakness |
|----------|----------|
| ✅ Excellent diction (20 years of experience) | ❌ 90% lacking breath support |
| ✅ Deep understanding of vocal technique | ❌ Undeveloped diaphragm/intercostals |
| ✅ Perfect pitch (trained ear) | ❌ Weak resonance sustainability |
| ✅ Emotional maturity | ❌ "Pushing" to compensate |

#### The Supercar Problem
> "Your vocalization is like a supercar engine given to a Suzuki Every Wagon."

His mind knows how to produce operatic power. His 17-year-old body cannot handle the stress. Must rebuild from foundation.

#### Psychological Profile
- **Post-Traumatic Success Disorder** — Even victories feel hollow
- **Adult in a teen world** — Finds peer drama trivial
- **Haunted by alcoholism** — 20 years of failure echo in every note
- **Cold exterior** — Maturity gap reads as arrogance, but internally full of dad jokes
- **Humor Style** — Dry, self-deprecating, and prone to terrible puns (Dad Jokes) that confuse his 17-year-old peers.
- **Hidden Passion** — Genuine motorcycle enthusiast. Loves the mechanics, the freedom, and the technical precision of riding (parallels his approach to vocal technique).

## The Three Rivals

### 1. Isabella "Bella" Aragon — The Prodigy

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Voice Type** | Soprano (Lyric) |
| **Age** | 17 |
| **Archetype** | The Silver Spoon |
| **Family** | Musical dynasty — mother is a famous Philippine soprano |

**Personality:**
- Technically flawless, emotionally hollow
- Raised to be perfect; never learned to feel music
- Ice-cold demeanor hides deep insecurity
- Believes talent is inherited, not earned

**Conflict with David:**
She represents everything David resents: privilege without struggle. But she's also a mirror — David's *own* arrogance before his fall.

**Character Arc Potential:**
Eventually forced to confront that technique without soul is empty.

---

### 2. Maricel "Cel" Pagtalunan — The Politician

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Voice Type** | Mezzo-Soprano |
| **Age** | 17 |
| **Archetype** | The Schemer |
| **Family** | Middle-class; clawed her way into SPA on scholarship |

**Personality:**
- Talented but not exceptional
- Compensates through manipulation and networking
- Knows every teacher's weakness, every judge's preference
- Friendly facade, calculating underneath

**Conflict with David:**
She sees David as an unpredictable variable threatening her careful plans. Will attempt to sabotage, ally, or seduce — whatever works.

**Character Arc Potential:**
Must choose between manufactured success and genuine artistry.

---

### 3. Rafael "Rafe" Montoya — The Mirror

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Voice Type** | Tenor (Spinto) |
| **Age** | 18 |
| **Archetype** | The Rival David Could Have Been |
| **Family** | Wealthy, supportive parents; every advantage |

**Personality:**
- Genuinely talented — the "chosen one" of the school
- Arrogant but not cruel
- Believes he's destined for greatness
- Has never tasted failure

**Conflict with David:**
Rafe occupies the spotlight David wants. Their rivalry is the central tension of Arc I. But David sees his old self in Rafe — the arrogance that led to his fall.

**Character Arc Potential:**
Must experience failure to become a true artist.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|

| **Role** | Vocal Technique Instructor, DCNHS-SPA |

> "I don't teach students to sound powerful. I teach them to *be* powerful. The sound will follow."

David blamed him for teaching "weak" techniques. Quit his tutelage. Regretted it for 20 years.

David now understands: Professor Villanueva was building a foundation. This time, he listens.

## Voice Type Reference

| Type | Range | Role in Opera |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Soprano** | C4-C6 | Heroine, Lead Female |
| **Mezzo-Soprano** | A3-A5 | Supporting Female, Often Complex Roles |
| **Tenor** | C3-C5 | Hero, Lead Male (Primo Uomo) |
| **Baritone** | A2-A4 | Supporting Male, Villains, Fathers |
| **Bass** | E2-E4 | Authority Figures, Villains, Comic Relief |

**David's Transition Challenge:**
From Baritone (supporting roles, villains) → Tenor (the hero, the star). The passaggio must shift from D4 to E4-A4.
